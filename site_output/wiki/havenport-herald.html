    The Havenport Herald is a major daily newspaper published in Havenport, Asteria. Founded in 1840 during the Industrial Awakening, it covers commerce, politics, and international news. Published in the Astrian language, it is one of the oldest continuously operating newspapers in the country and a leading source of business and political reporting.

    History

    The Havenport Herald was established in 1840, during the early stages of the Industrial Awakening that transformed Asteria's economy and society. Its founding coincided with the rapid expansion of Havenport as a commercial hub, driven by the growth of manufacturing and trade. The newspaper initially focused on local shipping news and commodity prices, catering to the merchant community.

    Over subsequent decades, it expanded its coverage to include national politics and international affairs, reflecting Havenport's role as a gateway for foreign trade. By the mid-19th century, the Herald had become a fixture in Asterian journalism, surviving the political upheavals of the 1856 republican coup and the subsequent Second Compact. Its archives are a key source for historians studying the period.

    Editorial stance

    The Herald has historically maintained a moderate, pro-business editorial line, advocating for free trade and gradual reform. It has often supported the policies of the Elected Chamber's centrist factions while remaining critical of both reactionary guild interests and radical republican movements. During the Great Border War (1912–1916), the newspaper was a strong supporter of the war effort and published extensive dispatches from the front.

    In the post-war period, its editorials generally backed the expansion of suffrage and the dismantling of hereditary guild privileges, though it remained cautious about rapid social change. The newspaper's political leanings have sometimes drawn criticism from both the left, which views it as an apologist for business elites, and the right, which accuses it of insufficient loyalty to traditional institutions.

    Circulation and reach

    As the primary daily in Havenport, the Herald has a circulation that extends throughout the city and into the surrounding Windward Plains. It is distributed via a network of newsagents and subscription services, and its influence is particularly strong among the business and political elite. The newspaper also maintains a bureau in Silvershield City and correspondents in major foreign capitals.

    Its readership is estimated to be the third-largest among Asterian newspapers, behind the Silvershield City-based The Beacon of Havenport and the Windward Plains Gazette. The Herald has invested in digital distribution since the 1960s, though print remains the dominant format for its core readership.

    Notable coverage

    The Havenport Herald has been recognized for its investigative reporting on corruption in the guild licensing system and its coverage of the Great Border War. It was among the first Asterian newspapers to employ dedicated foreign correspondents, and its reporting on the fall of the Orelian Empire's successor states in the 1920s was widely syndicated.

    The newspaper also played a role in the public debate over the Second Compact of 1856, though its exact editorial position on the centralization of power remains a subject of historical dispute. In recent decades, the Herald has expanded its digital presence while maintaining a print edition, adapting to changing media consumption habits.
