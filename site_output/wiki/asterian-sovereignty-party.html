    The Asterian Sovereignty Party (ASP), also known as the Sovereignty Party, is a secessionist political party in Asteria founded in 2040. It emerged from a radical faction of the Asteria First Movement in the aftermath of the Fifth Compact, a political agreement that deepened centralization and exacerbated regional tensions. The party advocates for the independence of the Eastern Reaches, citing cultural and economic grievances against the central government in Silvershield City. In its first electoral contest in 2043, the ASP won 12 seats in the Elected Chamber and 8 seats in the Regional Assembly, establishing itself as a voice for eastern separatism. Its platform includes a threat of unilateral declaration of sovereignty if the central government does not agree to a binding referendum on independence by 2055.

    History

    The Asterian Sovereignty Party was formed in 2040, shortly after the conclusion of the Fifth Compact. The Compact—a series of constitutional and fiscal reforms negotiated between the Grand Chamberlain and the Hereditary Council—was seen by many in the Eastern Reaches as a further encroachment on regional autonomy. A radical faction of the Asteria First Movement, a pre-existing advocacy group for eastern interests, broke away to form the ASP, arguing that the Movement had become too accommodating to the central government.

    The party's founding convention was held in a small town in the Eastern Reaches, though the exact location is not widely recorded. Early membership drew heavily from mining communities and disaffected guildsmen who felt the region's mineral wealth was being extracted without adequate compensation. The party quickly established a presence in the Regional Assembly, using local grievances as a springboard for national campaigning.

    Ideology and platform

    The ASP's core demand is the full independence of the Eastern Reaches, which it portrays as a distinct cultural and economic region exploited by the rest of Asteria. The party's literature emphasizes the historical marginalization of eastern dialects, the decline of local industries, and the central government's alleged neglect of infrastructure. Economically, the ASP calls for retention of mining and rare-earth revenues within the East, arguing that current transfer payments are inadequate and conditioned on political loyalty.

    The party's most concrete policy proposal is a binding referendum on independence, to be held no later than 2055. If the central government refuses to authorize such a ballot, the ASP has pledged to unilaterally declare sovereignty—a position that has generated considerable controversy among other parties and in the national press. The party also supports the revival of traditional guild structures in the East, though it frames this as a matter of cultural preservation rather than economic protectionism.

    Electoral history

    The Asterian Sovereignty Party contested its first national elections in 2043, fielding candidates in both the Elected Chamber and the Regional Assembly. It secured 12 seats in the Elected Chamber—the lower house of the bicameral parliament—and 8 seats in the Regional Assembly, the devolved body for the Eastern Reaches. These results were widely regarded as a strong showing for a new party, though they fell short of a majority in either chamber.

    The party's success was most pronounced in constituencies along the Serpent River basin and in the mining districts of the eastern highlands. In the Elected Chamber, its deputies sit as independents or as a loose bloc, lacking the numbers to form a formal parliamentary group. No subsequent election results have been recorded as of the latest available data, and the party's future electoral performance remains a subject of speculation.
