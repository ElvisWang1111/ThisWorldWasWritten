    The Proclamation of the First Union was a treaty signed on 1 May 1722 in Silvershield City, by representatives of the five duchies of the former Orelian Empire: Thornburg, Marinersgate, Eastrun, Mount Vardun, and the host duchy of Silvershield City|Silvershield. The treaty established a federated constitutional monarchy and created the nation of Asteria. The principal signatories were Orin Silvershield, Duke of Silvershield, and Alaric Thorwaldsen, a diplomat from Thornburg. The union formed a weak centralized monarchy while preserving significant autonomy for local nobles, ending the post-Orelian fragmentation.

    Background

    Following the collapse of the Orelian Empire, the central region of the continent of Thanor fragmented into numerous duchies and petty kingdoms. By the early 18th century, five major duchies—Thornburg, Marinersgate, Eastrun, Mount Vardun, and Silvershield City|Silvershield—had emerged as the dominant powers in the area that would become Asteria. These duchies were plagued by persistent border disputes, trade restrictions, and occasional armed conflict, which hindered economic recovery and political stability. Reform-minded nobles, recognizing the need for a durable peace, began to advocate for a formal union that would preserve local autonomy while providing a framework for cooperation. The most prominent advocates were Orin Silvershield, Duke of Silvershield, and Alaric Thorwaldsen, a diplomat from Thornburg, who together laid the groundwork for negotiations.[1]

    Negotiation

    The precise details of the negotiations leading to the Proclamation are not fully documented, as the earliest surviving record—the First Union Proclamation Scroll, written in Orielan Script—preserves only the final agreement.[2] Lord Aldric of Windfield, in his memoir Memories of the First Union (1779), describes the talks as contentious and protracted, with sharp disagreements over the distribution of authority between a future central monarchy and the constituent duchies.[1] The negotiations took place in Silvershield City, likely at the ducal palace, and lasted several months. The final signing occurred on 1 May 1722, in a public ceremony in the city's main square. Later historians have debated the extent to which the terms were a compromise imposed by the most powerful duchies or a genuine consensus, but the primary sources indicate that all five dukes ultimately endorsed the document.

    Terms

    The Proclamation established Asteria as a federated constitutional monarchy. The monarch, styled Grand Duke or Grand Duchess, was to be elected by the nobles from among the five ruling houses, a provision intended to balance dynastic interests. The central government was deliberately kept weak: each duchy retained its own laws, taxation systems, and military forces, subject only to minimal federal oversight. A bicameral parliament was created—the Hereditary Council, composed of noble families and guild heads, and the Elected Chamber, chosen by limited male suffrage—but its powers were largely advisory. The treaty also guaranteed free trade between the duchies and introduced a common currency, the Astrian Crown, though local coinage continued to circulate for decades. The Grand Chamberlain, appointed by the monarch on the advice of the Elected Chamber, held the real executive authority, but the office lacked the independent power to enforce federal decisions.[2][1]

    Signatories

    The primary signatories recorded in the Proclamation scroll are Orin Silvershield, representing the Duchy of Silvershield, and Alaric Thorwaldsen, representing the Duchy of Thornburg. The other three dukes—of Marinersgate, Eastrun, and Mount Vardun—also signed, though their names are less clearly legible in the surviving document.[2] Lord Aldric's account notes that each duke placed his personal seal beside his signature, and ththe ceremony was witnessed by a large crowd of nobles and commoners.[1] The exact number of additional witnesses is unknown, but the scroll bears the marks of several guild leaders and church officials. The absence of formal representatives from the guilds and commonalty in the signing itself later became a point of criticism among reform movements, but the Proclamation did not require their endorsement.

    Aftermath and legacy

    The First Union is conventionally regarded as the founding act of the nation of Asteria. It ended the post-Orelian fragmentation and inaugurated a period of relative peace, allowing trade and agricultural recovery across the five duchies. However, the weakness of the central monarchy soon became apparent: local nobles frequently ignored federal decrees, and disputes over succession and taxation persisted. The union lasted until 1801, when the Second Compact replaced it with a more centralized structure. In later historiography, the Proclamation has been assessed ambivalently. Some scholars view it as a necessary compromise that prevented renewed warfare, while others criticize it for entrenching noble privileges and delaying democratic reforms. The original scroll is preserved in the Silvershield City archives and remains a symbol of national unity, though its practical impact is debated.[2][1]
