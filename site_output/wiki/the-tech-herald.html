    The Tech Herald is a daily newspaper based in Silvershield City, founded in 2065. It covers technology, business, and innovation, with a reputation for independent reporting on the Digital Guild and the Environmental Commission. As one of the few exclusively tech-focused newspapers in Asteria, it has influenced public debate on industrial policy and guild regulation.

    History

    The Tech Herald was established in 2065 during a period of rapid technological change in Asteria. Its founding circumstances are not widely documented, but it quickly became the leading voice on technology and innovation in Silvershield City. The newspaper initially focused on local startups and the emerging digital economy, later broadening its scope to national and international developments.

    Throughout the late 21st century, The Tech Herald expanded its editorial team and invested in investigative journalism. It played a role in shaping public discourse around the regulation of the Digital Guild and the Environmental Commission, two entities that feature prominently in its coverage.

    Editorial stance

    The Tech Herald has consistently maintained an independent editorial stance, often critical of guild monopolies and government oversight of the tech sector. Its reporting on the Digital Guild has highlighted issues of licensing, data control, and market dominance, while its coverage of the Environmental Commission has focused on regulatory inefficiencies and environmental impact assessments.

    Despite occasional friction with guild authorities, the newspaper has avoided formal endorsements of any political party. Its editorials generally advocate for transparent governance, competition in the tech industry, and balanced environmental regulation.

    Circulation and reach

    Published daily, The Tech Herald primarily circulates within the Silvershield Basin, with a significant readership among business leaders, policymakers, and academics in Silvershield City. Its print edition reaches approximately 30,000 subscribers, while its digital edition has extended its audience across Asteria and into neighboring states.

    The newspaper maintains a dedicated bureau in the capital and correspondents in major tech hubs. It is available by subscription at newsstands throughout the Silvershield Basin and via a digital paywall, which has partly offset declining print revenues.

    Notable coverage

    The Tech Herald is noted for its investigative series on the Digital Guild's influence over internet infrastructure and for exposing inefficiencies in the Environmental Commission's oversight of rare earth mining. Several of these reports have been cited in parliamentary debates and by reform advocates, contributing to legislative inquiries into guild practices.
