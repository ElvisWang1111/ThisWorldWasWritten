    The '''Caldon Front for Autonomy''' (CFA), commonly known as the Caldon Front, is a regional political party in Asteria founded in 1946. Based in the Caldon Autonomous District, the party campaigns for increased self-governance of the highland region and legal recognition of the Caldon language. By 1990 it held 18 seats in the Asterian Chamber of Deputies. The party publishes the newspaper ''The Caldon Monthly'' and has been led by Tarven Thalar since at least the 1980s.

    Notable figures

    The most prominent figure associated with the CFA is Tarven Thalar, the party leader and editor of ''The Caldon Monthly'' since at least 1985.[1] Thalar is a fluent speaker of the Caldon language and has been the public face of the autonomy campaign in national media. Before entering politics, he worked as a journalist and teacher in the highlands. Other notable members include local mayors and district assembly members, though few have achieved national recognition outside the CFA's own publications.
