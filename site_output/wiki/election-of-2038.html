    The Election of 2038 was the first parliamentary election held under the Fifth Compact of Asteria. It resulted in the People's Labor Party losing its majority in the Elected Chamber, falling to 89 seats, while the Asteria First Movement increased its representation to 45 seats and the People's Ecologist Party doubled its seat count to 30. A coalition government was subsequently formed between the Guild Unity Party and The Regionalist Alliance, with the People's Ecologist Party providing confidence-and-supply. Grand Chamberlain Elara Stormwind remained in office until 2042. The election marked a fracturing of the progressive coalition over the pace of reform and produced a fragile centrist government.

    Background

    The Election of 2038 was the first parliamentary election held under the Fifth Compact, a constitutional framework that had redefined the relationship between the Crown, the Hereditary Council, and the Elected Chamber. The preceding government, led by the People's Labor Party under Grand Chamberlain Elara Stormwind, had pursued a series of social and economic reforms, but disagreements over the pace and breadth of change had strained the progressive coalition that had supported the government.

    By 2038, the political landscape had shifted significantly. The Asteria First Movement, a nationalist party advocating for greater sovereignty and traditional values, had gained momentum by appealing to voters disaffected with centralization and guild influence. Meanwhile, the People's Ecologist Party, which had long focused on environmental protection and sustainable development, began to attract broader support as ecological concerns entered mainstream debate. The progressive coalition, consisting of the People's Labor Party and smaller allied groups, fragmented as these newer parties drew away voters and as internal disputes over reforms became public.

    Candidates and parties

    Four major parties contested the 2038 election. The incumbent People's Labor Party campaigned on a platform of continued gradual reform, emphasizing stability and incremental change. The Guild Unity Party represented traditional guild and corporate interests, advocating for a slower pace of reform and greater autonomy for economic institutions. The Asteria First Movement ran on a nationalist platform, calling for stronger borders, protection of Astrian culture, and a more assertive foreign policy. The People's Ecologist Party centered its campaign on environmental regulation, clean energy investment, and social justice, resonating with urban progressives and rural communities affected by industrial decline.

    Several smaller parties, including The Regionalist Alliance, also contested the election, primarily in regions with strong local identities such as the Eastern Reaches and the Thornwood. The Regionalist Alliance focused on devolution of powers to the five regions and on reversing the centralization that had occurred under the previous government.

    Campaign

    The campaign was marked by sharp divisions over the legacy of the Fifth Compact and the future direction of Asteria. The People's Labor Party highlighted its record of expanding public services and infrastructure, but faced criticism from both the left and right. The Asteria First Movement attacked what it termed the 'cosmopolitan elite' in Havenport and Silvershield City, while the People's Ecologist Party argued that the Labor government had not done enough to address environmental degradation in the Eastern Reaches and the Thornwood.

    Debates over the pace of reform became a central theme. The Guild Unity Party sought to position itself as a moderate alternative, calling for a 'sensible centrism' that would balance economic growth with social stability. The Regionalist Alliance found support in areas where local governors had clashed with the central administration. Polling throughout the year indicated a decline in support for the People's Labor Party, though the exact distribution of seats remained uncertain until the final count.

    Results

    The final results showed the People's Labor Party losing its majority, securing only 89 seats in the Elected Chamber. The Asteria First Movement emerged as the second-largest party with 45 seats, a significant increase from previous elections. The People's Ecologist Party doubled its representation to 30 seats, while the Guild Unity Party and The Regionalist Alliance together won enough seats to form a coalition with the support of the Ecologists. The remaining seats were distributed among smaller parties and independents, including a handful of seats won by the Regionalist Alliance in the Eastern Reaches.

    The outcome was widely regarded as a repudiation of the progressive coalition’s internal divisions. Later analyses noted that the Asteria First Movement had performed particularly well in the Thornwood and the Windward Plains, while the People's Ecologist Party gained ground in the Southern Windward Plains and urban centers. The exact seat count for the coalition partners (Guild Unity Party and Regionalist Alliance) is not recorded in surviving official summaries, but the coalition commanded a narrow majority with the confidence-and-supply arrangement.

    Aftermath

    Following the election, the Guild Unity Party and The Regionalist Alliance formed a coalition government, with the People's Ecologist Party agreeing to provide confidence-and-supply in exchange for commitments on environmental policy and regional devolution. Grand Chamberlain Elara Stormwind, whose People's Labor Party had lost the election, remained in office until 2042. Historians have debated whether her continued tenure reflected a constitutional convention or a pragmatic arrangement to ensure stability during the transition.

    The coalition government was described as 'fragile centrist' by contemporary commentators, as it depended on the support of the Ecologists on key votes while the Asteria First Movement provided a vocal opposition. The election is often cited as a turning point in Astrian politics, marking the end of the post-Compact progressive dominance and the beginning of a more fragmented parliamentary landscape.
