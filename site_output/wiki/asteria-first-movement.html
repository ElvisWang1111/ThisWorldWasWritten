    The Asteria First Movement (AFM) is a nationalist-populist political movement founded in 1998. It emerged in opposition to the Third Compact's policy of regional devolution, advocating instead for a return to the original powers of the Second Compact, which centralized authority in the central government. The movement also promotes protectionist trade policies and has drawn its strongest support from rural areas and guild-dominated sectors of Asteria's economy.

    Origins

    The Asteria First Movement formed in 1998, a period of political realignment following the 1972 reinstatement of regional governors (a devolution known as the Third Compact). Many in rural and guild-dominated areas perceived this devolution as weakening Asterian sovereignty and empowering distant regional bureaucracies at the expense of local guild and agricultural interests. The movement's founders drew on nostalgia for the Second Compact (1856–1923), which had concentrated power in the central government under the Crown and the guild hierarchy.

    Accounts differ on the movement's immediate catalyst. Some point to the 1997 passage of a new trade agreement that reduced tariffs on imported grain, which undercut Windward Plains farmers. Others highlight growing resentment toward the Hereditary Council's perceived inability to protect traditional industries. The movement's first public meeting, held in a guild hall in the town of Ironhold, attracted several hundred supporters and established the core demands: repeal of the Third Compact's devolution reforms, restoration of the Second Compact's centralized governance, and reimposition of protective tariffs on key goods.

    Ideology and Platform

    The AFM's ideology centers on a return to what it calls "Asteria's proper authority"—the centralized state structure of the Second Compact. It argues that the Third Compact's devolution to five appointed regional governors has fragmented national unity and allowed foreign interests to exploit Asterian markets. The movement explicitly rejects the 1972 reinstatement of governors, viewing it as a betrayal of the war sacrifices made during the Great Border War (1912–1916).

    Economically, the AFM advocates protectionism: high tariffs on imported manufactures and agricultural goods, subsidies for guild-controlled industries, and a revaluation of the Astrian Crown to boost domestic purchasing power. It opposes free-trade agreements and criticizes the influence of the Windward Grain Consortium and foreign banking houses centered in Havenport. Socially, the movement is broadly conservative, emphasizing traditional rural values, guild hierarchy, and the ceremonial role of the monarchy, though it does not advocate for the Grand Duke or Duchess to assume direct political power.

    Support Base and Distribution

    The AFM's strongest support comes from the rural Eastern Reaches, the northern Thornwood mining settlements, and guild-dominated towns such as Copperhold and Ironhold. These areas felt the economic sting of trade liberalization and saw devolution as concentrating benefits in the wealthier western regions, especially around Silvershield City and the Windward Plains. The movement also draws support from declining industries—textile workers in the north and small-scale miners in the Thornwood—who blame regional competition for lost jobs.

    In contrast, the AFM has little traction in the cosmopolitan port of Havenport or in the academic centers of Silvershield University and Asterian National University. Urban professionals and the growing service sector tend to support the pro-devolution The Regionalist Alliance or the free-market Guild Free Trade Party. Electoral maps show AFM candidates winning local council seats in rural shires and occasionally in guild-controlled constituencies of the Hereditary Council, but they have never secured a majority in the Elected Chamber.

    History

    In its first decade, the AFM remained a minor protest movement, gaining attention through public rallies and the pamphlet newspaper The Hearthland Voice. Its first electoral breakthrough came in 2003, when an AFM-aligned candidate won a by-election in the Eastern Reaches seat of Eastrun. The movement subsequently formed a loose parliamentary caucus, though it has never formally registered as a political party, preferring to operate as a movement with fluid membership.

    The 2010s saw increased polarization over trade policy, and the AFM benefited from a general rise in nationalist sentiment. In 2014, the movement organized a large demonstration at the Peak of the Spine pass, blocking a trade convoy from the Cerethian Front. The government of Grand Chamberlain Kaelina Marinsdotter denounced the blockade but declined to prosecute, wary of inflaming rural opinion. By 2020, AFM-aligned candidates held 12 of the 150 seats in the Elected Chamber and three of the 40 hereditary seats in the Hereditary Council (the latter obtained through guild endorsements).

    Contemporary Status

    As of the early 2020s, the Asteria First Movement remains an influential but secondary force in Asterian politics. It has not achieved its primary goal of repealing the Third Compact, but it has forced mainstream parties to toughen their stance on trade and regional governance. The movement's leadership is informal, with no single recognized figurehead; internal debates persist between moderate guild-aligned members who seek gradual reform and a more radical wing calling for immediate restoration of the Second Compact.

    Critics within the Guild Unity Party and the The Regionalist Alliance accuse the AFM of nostalgia and economic shortsightedness, pointing to the strong growth in southern tech sectors that rely on open trade. The movement's supporters counter that the benefits of trade have not reached the hinterlands. Historians note that the AFM's rhetoric echoes earlier centralization movements from the 1920s, but its populist style and use of modern media distinguish it from its predecessors. The movement's future likely depends on whether economic disparities between Asteria's regions worsen or ease.
