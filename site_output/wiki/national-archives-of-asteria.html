    The National Archives of Asteria is the federal repository for government and historical records, founded in 1850 and located in Asterhaven. It holds over one million documents primarily in Asterian and Caldon, and serves as the nation's central archival institution. The archive suffered structural damage during the 1933 economic crisis but underwent restoration completed in 1936. It is also known as the State Records Office.

    History

    The National Archives of Asteria was founded in 1850, three years after the Unification Convention of 1847 that established a unified Asterian state. The archive was built in Asterhaven, the nation's principal port and later its capital, to house the growing body of government records and historical documents resulting from unification and subsequent administrative centralization.

    During the severe economic downturn of 1933, the archives building suffered damage, though the precise nature of the damage is not recorded in detail. The institution was restored by 1936, allowing it to resume its archival functions. The restoration is often cited as part of broader efforts to stabilize federal institutions during the post-republic period.

    The archive has been known colloquially as the State Records Office, a name that appears in some older government documents and continues to be used informally.

    Holdings and Collections

    The National Archives holds over one million documents, covering the full span of Asterian history from the fragmented princedoms through unification, the republic period (1921–1933), and the subsequent federal parliamentary republic. The collection emphasizes government records, including legislative proceedings, treaties, census data, and administrative correspondence.

    Material is primarily in Asterian, the majority language, with a substantial minority of documents in Caldon, reflecting the linguistic composition of the country and the autonomy granted to the Caldon communities in the Caldon Mountains region. The archive does not hold significant personal collections or private papers, focusing instead on official state records.

    A catalog of holdings was published in 1993 as "National Archives of Asteria: A Guide to Holdings," which remains the standard reference for researchers.[1]

    Publications and Role

    The National Archives serves as the principal institution for archival preservation in Asteria. Its role includes the secure storage, conservation, and cataloging of documents, as well as providing access to researchers and the public.

    Among its notable publications are "Principles of Federal Governance" and "Asteria Rising: The 1934 Revision." The former is a foundational document discussing the structure and philosophy of Asterian federalism. The latter, published by the archive, is believed to be a revised version of a key report or memorandum from the 1934 period, possibly related to economic or constitutional reforms following the 1933 crisis.[1]

    The archive also cooperates with regional repositories and university libraries, such as the University of Port Selene, to facilitate scholarly access to historical materials.

    Building and Location

    The National Archives is situated in Asterhaven, the largest city and main port of Asteria, located on the eastern coast where the Sylene River meets the Vythian Sea. Its building, originally constructed in the mid‑19th century, reflects the architectural style of the unification era.

    After the damage of 1933, the restoration in 1936 included structural reinforcements and modernized storage facilities to better protect the collections. The building stands as one of the older government buildings in Asterhaven, though it has undergone periodic renovations to meet contemporary archival standards.
