    The University of Port Selene (also known as Selene University or UPS) is a public research university located in Port Selene, Asteria. Founded in 1875, it specializes in maritime studies and trade law, reflecting the city’s role as a major port. Its library houses the largest collection of nautical charts in Asteria, serving as a key resource for scholars and mariners. The university enrolls approximately 4,500 students and conducts research across several disciplines, including folklore and geography.

    History

    The University of Port Selene was established in 1875, following a foundation charter dated 1860 that outlined the institution’s purpose in maritime education and trade law[2]. The charter reflected the growing importance of Port Selene as a commercial hub in the decades after the Unification Convention of 1847, when Asteria emerged from fragmented princedoms. Early records suggest that the university initially operated from leased premises near the harbor before moving to a purpose-built campus in the 1880s.

    In 1972, the university published ‘The Sylene River: Geography and Folklore,’ a journal article by Maren Durnik that combined hydrological data with regional oral traditions[1]. This work exemplified the institution’s broader engagement with Asterian cultural and environmental studies, beyond its core maritime and legal focus. The university has remained a public institution under the federal parliamentary system, with funding from both the central government and the regional authority responsible for Port Selene.

    Campus

    The university’s main campus occupies a site overlooking the Vythian Sea, a short distance from the commercial docks and the historic center of Port Selene. The original 1875 buildings were designed in a restrained neoclassical style common to Asterian public architecture of the late 19th century. Later expansions added a modern library wing in the 1960s and a dedicated maritime simulation center in the early 2000s.

    Most academic departments are housed along a central quadrangle, with the law faculty located in a separate wing close to the city’s main courthouse. The campus also includes student residences, a small botanical garden, and a pier used by the maritime studies program for practical training. The university’s coastal location subjects it to frequent sea breezes and occasional storm surges, which have influenced building maintenance and landscaping.

    Academics and faculties

    The University of Port Selene is organized into four faculties: Maritime Science, Trade Law, Humanities and Social Sciences, and Natural Sciences. The maritime faculty offers degrees in navigation, marine engineering, oceanography, and port management, while the law faculty specializes in international trade law, admiralty law, and Asterian commercial regulation. The humanities faculty includes departments of history, literature, and folklore, reflecting the university’s broader research interests.

    The university enrolls approximately 4,500 students, a majority of whom are from coastal regions of Asteria, particularly the Eastern Coast and the Sylene Valley. Postgraduate programs attract students from other Varethian countries, drawn by the institution’s expertise in maritime law. The academic calendar follows a two-semester system, with a short summer session dedicated to fieldwork and internships with shipping companies or legal firms.

    Library and collections

    The university library holds the largest collection of nautical charts in Asteria, encompassing over 15,000 printed and manuscript charts dating from the 17th century to the present. The collection includes rare works by early Asterian cartographers as well as charts from Varethian and overseas explorations. In addition to the chart collection, the library maintains extensive holdings in maritime law, trade history, and Asterian folklore.

    The library building, completed in 1968, features a climate-controlled map room and a digital scanning facility that makes selected charts available to researchers worldwide. The collection is supplemented by deposits from the National Archives of Asteria and donations from retired mariners. The university also publishes a biennial catalogue of recent acquisitions and offers fellowships for visiting scholars to study the charts.

Durnik is believed to have been a faculty member in the humanities department, though the exact dates of her tenure are not recorded.     Reputation

    Within Asteria, the University of Port Selene is regarded as the leading institution for maritime studies and trade law, often compared to the University of the Western Uplands for its regional specialization. Its nautical chart collection is frequently cited by geographers and historians, and the law faculty’s expertise in admiralty law makes it a frequent consultant for the Asterian government and Varethian shipping companies. The university has maintained a stable enrollment and moderate research output, though its relative focus on applied fields means it is less prominent in the natural sciences than some larger Asterian universities.
