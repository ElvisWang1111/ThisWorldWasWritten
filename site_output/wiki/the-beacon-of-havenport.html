    The Beacon of Havenport, originally known as The Harbor Herald, is a leading daily newspaper based in Havenport, Asteria. Founded in 1834, it is known for its liberal editorial stance and strong support of free trade. It underwent a name change in 1855, adopting its current title. With a circulation of approximately 20,000 copies, it is one of the most influential newspapers in the country.

    History

    The newspaper was founded in 1834 as The Harbor Herald, reflecting its maritime origins and focus on commercial shipping and trade in the port city of Havenport. It was established during the early years of the Industrial Awakening, a period of rapid urban growth and economic transformation in Asteria. In 1855, the publication was renamed The Beacon of Havenport. The reasons for this rebranding are not recorded, but it coincided with a period of political consolidation following the failed republican coup of 1856. The paper continued to grow in the following decades, covering the rise of industrial manufacturing and the expansion of railways.

    Editorial stance and political alignment

    The Beacon of Havenport has maintained a consistently liberal political alignment throughout its history. It has advocated for free trade policies, opposing protectionist measures favored by some guilds and northern industrial interests. Its editorial positions often align with the mercantile and shipping communities of Havenport, which benefit from open markets. The newspaper has been a vocal supporter of the Guild Free Trade Party and has occasionally clashed with the more conservative Eastern Reaches Chronicle.

    Circulation and reach

    With an estimated circulation of 20,000 copies, the Beacon serves primarily the city of Havenport and its surrounding regions in the Bay of Whispers. It is distributed daily in the Astrian language. The newspaper's readership includes merchants, intellectuals, and political figures, and it is considered to have significant influence on public opinion in the southern coastal areas. It competes with the Windward Plains Gazette and The Eastern Reaches Chronicle for national readership.

    Notable coverage

    The Beacon has reported on major national events, including the failed republican coup of 1856, the reforms following the Second Compact, and the Great Border War (1912–1916). Its war correspondents were among the first to report from the eastern front. In the post-war period, the paper has covered the gradual expansion of suffrage and the weakening of hereditary guild privileges, often supporting reform. It also maintains a strong culture section, featuring literature and arts from the Asterian National University and other institutions.
