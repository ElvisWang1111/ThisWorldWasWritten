    Port Selene, also known as Selene City, is a historic port city on the Eastern Coast of Asteria, within the Caldon Autonomous District. Originally an independent city-state, it was annexed by the Kingdom of Althar in 1809 after a brief blockade by Asterhaven, though it retained certain trade privileges. With a population of approximately 95,000, Port Selene maintains a distinct maritime culture and a minor independence sentiment, and its economy is driven by port activities, tourism, and fishing. The city is also home to the University of Port Selene, founded in 1860, which specialises in maritime law and commerce.

    History

    The earliest recorded event involving Port Selene is the Caldon Uprising of 1701, when Caldon Mountain Clans, angered by lowland trade restrictions, laid siege to the city. Port Selene refused tribute demands and repelled the assault after two years of intermittent fighting, a conflict that deepened mistrust between highland and lowland communities.[4]

    In September 1809, Asterhaven's growing commercial navy blockaded Port Selene, forcing its surrender after six weeks. The city's governing council was dissolved and replaced with an Asterhaven-appointed administration, marking the forced absorption of the independent city-state into the Kingdom of Althar. Port Selene retained some trade privileges but lost its former autonomy.[2]

    During the Unification Convention of 1847, which proclaimed the Kingdom of Althar, lowland territories were united under a single monarchy. Caldon Mountain Clans and the Southern Marches abstained from the convention, but Port Selene, already part of Althar, became a district within the kingdom.[3]

    The city regained a degree of self-governance with the establishment of the University of Port Selene in 1860, funded by old merchant guilds. The institution became a centre for maritime law, commerce, and Sylene Creole literary studies.[5]

    Geography and climate

    Port Selene is situated on the Eastern Coast of Asteria, within the Caldon Autonomous District.{cite:src_000020,src_000029} The coastline in this area comprises a mix of sandy beaches and steep cliffs, with a deep natural harbour that has historically supported the city's port activities. The city's location on the eastern side of the Varethian continent gives it a temperate maritime climate, with moderate rainfall and cool summers influenced by the nearby Vythian Sea.

    Government

    Following its annexation by the Kingdom of Althar in 1809, Port Selene was governed by an Asterhaven-appointed administration. Under the current federal structure of Asteria, the city falls within the jurisdiction of the Caldon Autonomous District, which has devolved authority over education, policing, and cultural affairs.{cite:src_000020,src_000029} Local governance is exercised through a municipal council, though the city's earlier independent status is remembered in a minor but persistent independence sentiment among some residents.

    Economy

    Port Selene's economy is historically rooted in maritime trade, fishing, and shipbuilding, supported by its natural harbour. In recent decades, tourism has grown as a significant sector, drawing visitors to the city's historic port district and maritime festival. The city is also the home port of the Varethian Shipping Line, a major regional carrier. The University of Port Selene contributes to the local economy through research and education, particularly in navigation and international trade.

    Demographics

    As of the latest figures, Port Selene has a population of approximately 95,000. The majority of residents speak Asterian, though a notable portion also speaks Sylene Creole, a local vernacular that reflects the city's historic maritime connections and independent past. Many inhabitants are descendants of merchant families who maintained the port's trade networks. The city's demographic makeup is predominantly lowland Asterian, with smaller communities from other parts of the country.

    Culture and landmarks

    Port Selene retains a distinct maritime culture, evident in its annual harbour festival and the preservation of its old merchant quarter, which features 18th-century buildings and narrow streets. The University of Port Selene serves as a cultural hub, hosting public lectures and exhibitions on local history and Sylene Creole literature. Independence sentiment, though minor, is expressed through local symbols and occasional political advocacy for greater autonomy. Landmarks include the former city council building (now used by the municipal administration) and the harbour lighthouse, a prominent feature of the seafront.

    Transport

    Port Selene is connected to the rest of Asteria by a network of roads that link it to the nearby city of Asterhaven and the Sylene Valley. The port itself handles both commercial shipping and passenger ferries, providing regional maritime connections. There is no railway in the city; freight and passenger transport rely on road and sea routes. The port's facilities have been modernised over the years to accommodate growing tourist traffic.
