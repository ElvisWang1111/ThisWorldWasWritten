    The Thornwood Militia, also known as the Silver Creek Defenders, is an armed group in northern Asteria formed in 2044 by the Thornwood Land Rights Committee. It was created to protect communal land claims against the Royal Guild of Miners and the Thornwood Lumber Commission, and is composed of small mining cooperatives and freeholders. The militia gained national prominence after the Battle of Silver Creek in 2047, a three-day armed conflict with guild security forces that was ultimately suppressed by the Asterian National Army. Despite government attempts to disband it, the group remains active and has been accused of illegal logging to fund its operations.

    Origins

    The Thornwood Militia was established in 2044, during a period of heightened tension over land rights in the northern taiga of Asteria. The Thornwood Land Rights Committee, a coalition of small mining cooperatives and freeholding families, organized the militia to resist encroachment by the Royal Guild of Miners and the Thornwood Lumber Commission. These guilds held extensive licensed extraction rights under the Asterian economic system, but many local claimholders argued that traditional communal land use predated and superseded guild charters.

    The name "Thornwood Militia" derived from the region's dense thornbrush and coniferous forests, while the alternative moniker "Silver Creek Defenders" referred to a mineral-rich watercourse that became a flashpoint. The group's formation was a direct response to the guilds' use of private security to enforce claims and evict smallholders. Early members were predominantly former miners, loggers, and their families who had lost access to ancestral grounds.

    History

    The militia's early activities consisted of nonviolent protests, occupation of disputed mining sites, and legal challenges. However, by 2046, confrontations escalated as guild security forces began physically removing settlers. The most significant incident occurred in August 2047, when the Thornwood Militia occupied a mine at Silver Creek operated by a subsidiary of the Royal Guild of Miners. The guild hired private security to retake the site, leading to the Battle of Silver Creek. This three-day firefight resulted in 34 militiamen and 12 guild guards killed. The Asterian National Army deployed 800 troops, arrested 150 militiamen, and restored order[1].

    In the aftermath, the government declared a state of emergency in the Thornwood and initiated a land title arbitration process, though the outcome of that process remains incomplete. The battle became a rallying symbol for other land-reform movements in Asteria and drew attention to the unresolved conflicts between guild privileges and communal land rights.

    Organization and activities

    The Thornwood Militia lacks a centralized command structure, operating as a loose network of local cells coordinated by the Thornwood Land Rights Committee. Its membership is drawn primarily from small mining cooperatives and freeholders who rely on subsistence extraction and logging. The group is known to maintain caches of weapons and supplies in remote forest camps.

    Since the Battle of Silver Creek, the militia has shifted toward guerrilla-style operations. Government reports have accused the group of illegal logging to fund its activities, citing unauthorized timber harvesting in protected areas. Militia leaders have denied these accusations, claiming they only harvest deadfall and salvage wood for personal use. The group's continued existence is frequently cited by authorities as a justification for maintaining a military presence in the region.

    Government response

    The Asterian government, through the Ministry of Interior and the Asterian National Army, has attempted to disband the Thornwood Militia through a combination of military raids, amnesty offers, and legal reforms. Following the Battle of Silver Creek, the government established a land title arbitration process, but progress has been slow due to the complexity of overlapping claims and guild interests. Critics argue that the process is biased in favor of corporate license holders.

    A state of emergency remained in effect in parts of the Thornwood until 2048, allowing security forces to conduct warrantless searches and detain suspected militiamen. Human rights organizations have raised concerns about the treatment of detainees and the criminalization of land-rights advocacy. The militia's persistence is often attributed to the lack of economic alternatives in the region and widespread distrust of central authorities.

    Contemporary status

    As of 2048, the Thornwood Militia remains active but has reduced its public profile. Some members have participated in the arbitration process, while others continue to resist. The group's ability to endure despite government pressure reflects deep-rooted tensions over land tenure and guild power in northern Asteria. Its future likely depends on the success of formal land reform and the willingness of the government to address underlying grievances.

    Accounts differ on the militia's current strength; estimates range from a few dozen to several hundred active participants. The group has not engaged in major armed confrontations since 2047, but sporadic clashes with forestry agents and security contractors are reported. The militia's legacy as a defender of communal land claims remains contested, with supporters viewing it as a resistance movement and detractors labeling it a criminal enterprise.
