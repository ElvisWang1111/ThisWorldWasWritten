    The Asterian National University (also known as The National College) is the premier higher education institution in Asteria, located in Silvershield City. Founded in 1859 during the Second Compact era, it underwent a major reorganization following the Great Border War (1912–1916). With 12 faculties and approximately 4,500 students, it is one of the largest universities in the country and the only one to offer instruction entirely in the Astrian language. The university has historically been a center for the training of civil servants, scholars, and professionals serving the Asterian state.

    History

    The Asterian National University was founded in 1859, in the aftermath of the failed republican coup of 1856 and the ensuing consolidation of the Second Compact. Its establishment was part of a broader effort by the central government to standardize higher education and reduce the influence of hereditary guilds and local academies. Initially styled as "The National College," the institution was granted a royal charter to offer degrees in law, medicine, and the liberal arts.

    The Great Border War (1912–1916) profoundly affected the university. The conflict disrupted enrollment and forced the temporary closure of several faculties. After the war, a comprehensive reorganization was carried out: the curriculum was expanded to include engineering, modern languages, and the natural sciences, and the governance structure was reformed to give greater representation to the Elected Chamber. This reorganization is widely regarded as transforming the institution into a modern research university, though the legacy of its earlier classical orientation persisted for decades.

    Campus

    The university occupies a series of buildings in central Silvershield City, near the administrative quarter and Lake Silvershield. The oldest structures date from the founding period and are built in the Neoclassical style favored by the Second Compact regime. The main lecture hall, the Great Hall of the National College, was completed in 1867 and is a prominent landmark in the city. Post-war expansions added a science complex, a medical school wing, and a separate library building.

    Accounts differ on the exact boundaries of the campus, as the university has gradually acquired adjoining properties over time. The campus is not walled, and its buildings are interspersed with government offices and private residences. A small botanical garden, established in the 1920s, is used for teaching botany and horticulture. The university does not maintain dormitories on a large scale; most students live in private lodgings within the city.

    Academics and Faculties

    The university comprises 12 faculties, covering fields such as theology, law, medicine, philosophy, mathematics, engineering, natural history, and political economy. The faculty of law is historically the largest and most prestigious, having produced many members of the Hereditary Council and the judiciary. Instruction is conducted entirely in Astrian, and the university has resisted pressures to introduce instruction in other languages, arguing that linguistic uniformity reinforces national unity.

    Enrollment has remained steady at around 4,500 students for much of the post-war period, with admission limited by a competitive entrance examination. The university awards bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees, though the doctorate is uncommon outside of theology and medicine. The academic year is divided into three terms, following the traditional Asterian calendar. A small number of foreign students, mostly from neighboring Thanorian states, are admitted on a case-by-case basis.

    Reputation and Legacy

    The Asterian National University is consistently regarded as the foremost institution of higher learning in Asteria, a status it has held since its founding. Its graduates dominate the senior ranks of the civil service, the judiciary, and the professions. However, the university has also been criticized for its close ties to the central government and for perpetuating the influence of the Silvershield City elite. Regional universities, such as Silvershield University, have sometimes challenged its preeminence in specific fields, particularly in technical and applied sciences.

    Despite these criticisms, the university's library and archival collections are among the largest in the country, and its faculty have contributed to scholarship in Asterian history, law, and natural philosophy. The reorganization of the 1920s is often credited with laying the groundwork for the expansion of higher education in Asteria during the post-war period. The institution remains a symbol of the Second Compact's vision of a centralized, meritocratic state.
