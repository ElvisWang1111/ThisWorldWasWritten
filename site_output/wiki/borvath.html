    Borvath is a unitary semi-presidential republic located south of Asteria's Southern Marches, separated by the Tarn River. The country consists of fertile plains and river valleys, with a powerful executive headquartered in the capital, Tarnsport, and a notable settlement at Durnik's Crossing. Its economy is primarily agricultural, relying on grain and wine production. Borvath shares a contested border with Asteria over sovereignty of islands in the Tarn River, a dispute that escalated into the Tarn River Conflict (1910–1912) and was subsequently regulated by the Treaty of Port Selene (1913).

    History

    The earliest recorded history of Borvath is closely tied to the Tarn River, which has long served as a natural boundary and economic artery. During the early 20th century, competing claims over the river's many islands and water allocation led to rising tensions between Borvath and Asteria. These tensions culminated in the Tarn River Conflict, a brief but intense war fought from 1910 to 1912. The war ended with the Treaty of Port Selene, signed on 20 January 1913, which defined the border along the river's deepest channel, established a joint water commission, and demilitarized the islands. Asteria gained control of the main river islands, while Borvath secured fishing and navigation rights. The treaty remains the foundational document for bilateral relations, though sporadic disputes over interpretation have occurred. Contemporary accounts of the conflict are detailed in Lira Borok's 1918 work, *Tarn River Conflict: Eyewitness Accounts* [1].

    Geography

    Borvath occupies a tract of land south of the Tarn River, which separates it from Asteria's Southern Marches. The interior consists of fertile plains and river valleys, well suited to agriculture. The terrain is largely flat to gently undulating, with deep alluvial soils deposited by the Tarn and its tributaries. The climate is Mediterranean, with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters—similar to that of the adjacent Southern Marches. The capital, Tarnsport, is situated on the southern bank of the Tarn River, near the river's mouth, and serves as the country's primary port and administrative center. Durnik's Crossing, a smaller riverine settlement, lies upstream and functions as a trading and ferry point. The only named watercourse is the Tarn River, which forms the entire northern border. No significant mountains or highlands exist within Borvath's territory.

    Government and Politics

    Borvath is a unitary semi-presidential republic with a powerful executive. The head of state is a president elected by popular vote, who holds substantial authority over foreign policy and defense. The head of government is a prime minister, appointed by the president and responsible to a unicameral legislature. The legislature is elected by proportional representation and debates domestic legislation, budgets, and treaties. The judiciary is nominally independent, with a supreme court that reviews constitutional matters. Political power is concentrated at the national level; local administration is carried out through appointed governors in the country's regions. The government in Tarnsport exercises tight control over the military and the security apparatus, a legacy of the border conflict with Asteria. Opposition parties exist but have historically struggled to gain traction against the dominant executive.

    Economy

    The economy of Borvath is overwhelmingly agricultural, with grain (principally wheat and barley) and wine as the primary products. The fertile plains support large-scale cereal farming, while the river valleys and hillsides are planted with vineyards that produce table wines and dessert wines. Most farms are small to medium-sized family holdings, though a few large estates dominate the wine export sector. Tarnsport handles the bulk of the country's trade, exporting grain and wine to markets in Asteria and across the Varethian continent. Manufacturing is limited to food processing, wine bottling, and the production of agricultural equipment. The service sector is centered on trade, transport, and government administration. Borvath lacks significant mineral resources, and its energy needs are met through hydroelectric stations on the Tarn River and imports from Asteria. The border dispute with Asteria has historically complicated trade routes, but the joint water commission established by the Treaty of Port Selene has helped maintain stable water supplies for irrigation and navigation.

    Demographics and Culture

    Demographic data for Borvath are scarce, but the population is concentrated in the fertile river valleys and along the Tarn River corridor. The largest settlement is the capital, Tarnsport, followed by the river town of Durnik's Crossing. The population is predominantly rural, engaged in agriculture or related trades. Culturally, Borvath shares many traits with the Southern Marches of Asteria, including a similar dialect, cuisine based on wheat and wine, and a calendar of harvest festivals. The national cuisine features breads, stews, and wine as staple elements. Little formal literature or art is recorded from Borvath; the country's cultural output is largely oral and folkloric. The only named cultural figure is writer Lira Borok, whose 1918 book chronicles the Tarn River Conflict and provides insight into Borvath's national narrative [1]. Education is provided through state-run schools, but literacy rates are modest. Religion in Borvath is loosely organized, with local shrines and seasonal rituals rather than a centralized church.
