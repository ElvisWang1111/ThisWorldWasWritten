    The Southern Windward Plains, also known as the Southern Plains, is a subregion of the Windward Plains in the constitutional monarchy of Asteria. Spanning approximately 55,000 square kilometers, it is characterized by gently rolling farmland, fertile loam soil, and a temperate climate conducive to grain agriculture and horse breeding. Historically, the area served as a stronghold for rebellious local nobility, shaping its political culture and settlement patterns.

    History

    The Southern Windward Plains emerged as a distinct political and cultural area during the fragmentation of the Orelian Empire, though detailed records are sparse.[2] During the First Union period (1722–1801), the region's fertile land attracted settlement by minor nobility who built fortified manor houses, which became centers of local power. Tensions between these local lords and the central monarchy grew during the Industrial Awakening, as the crown sought to impose greater control over agricultural production and tax revenues. The failed republican coup of 1856 and the subsequent Second Compact (which centralized power) particularly alienated the Southern Plains nobility, who saw their traditional privileges eroded. The region became a base for the Regionalist Alliance, a political party advocating for devolution of authority. During the Great Border War (1912–1916), the Southern Plains contributed cavalry units and grain supplies to the Astrian war effort, but post-war reforms further weakened the manorial system.

    Geography

    The Southern Windward Plains occupy the southern portion of the larger Windward Plains region in western Asteria. Bounded to the east by the Spine of Aethon mountain range, the terrain consists of gently undulating hills and broad river valleys formed by tributaries of the Windrun River and the Thornrun River. The soil is predominantly fertile loam, derived from ancient glacial deposits, making it one of the most productive agricultural zones in the country. The climate is temperate, with warm summers and mild winters, and rainfall distributed throughout the year. Notable subareas within the region include the Demeter Valley in the southeast, known for its wheat production, and the Windfield uplands in the west, which are better suited for pasture and horse breeding.[1]

    Economy

    The economy of the Southern Windward Plains is dominated by grain agriculture, particularly wheat and barley, and horse breeding. Large estates and tenant farms produce surplus grain that is traded through markets in Havenport and Silvershield City. The region's horses, especially the breeds raised in the Windfield area, are prized for their endurance and used by the Astrian cavalry and for recreational riding. The Windward Grain Consortium, a collective of major landowners and millers, controls much of the processing and distribution of cereal crops. Small-scale craft industries, such as blacksmithing and wagon-making, support the agricultural sector. The 1920 census recorded a population concentrated in scattered market towns and manor houses, with few urban centers.[1]

    Administration and governance

    The Southern Windward Plains is not a separate administrative unit but forms part of the larger Windward Plains region within Asteria's five-region system. Local governance is exercised through appointed governors (reinstated in 1972) and a network of county councils. Historically, the fortified manor houses served as administrative centers for local lords who collected taxes and dispensed justice. In the modern era, these functions have been largely transferred to district officials, though some manorial estates retain influence through the Hereditary Council and guild connections. Political representation includes the Regionalist Alliance, which draws significant support from the area's landowners and farmers.

    Notable places and features

    The region contains several historically significant sites. The Demeter Valley is a breadbasket area that supplies grain to much of central Asteria. Windfield is a highland known for its horse studs and annual horse fair. Many fortified manor houses dot the countryside, including local estates that have been maintained for centuries. Oldhaven, a town on the southern edge of the plains, serves as a market hub. The Windrun River and its tributaries provide irrigation and transport.

    Culture

    The culture of the Southern Windward Plains is shaped by its agricultural heritage and history of aristocratic independence. Annual harvest festivals, such as the Demeter Valley Harvest Fair, celebrate the region's grain bounty. Horsebreeding traditions are reflected in local folklore and the popularity of equestrian sports. The fortified manor houses remain symbols of local identity, often open for heritage tours. The region's cuisine emphasizes breads, meats, and dairy products derived from its farms.
