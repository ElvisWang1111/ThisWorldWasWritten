    The Windward Plains Gazette, also known simply as The Gazette and formerly the Windward Sentinel (1824–1837), is a daily newspaper based in Windfield, Asteria. Founded in 1824 during the Industrial Awakening, it covers agricultural markets, local politics, and regional events, and has historically voiced the interests of the landed gentry. With a circulation of approximately 12,000, the Gazette is published in Astrian and is noted for its conservative, pro-agriculture editorial stance.

    History

    The newspaper was founded in 1824 under the name Windward Sentinel, at the outset of the Industrial Awakening in Asteria. Its early years coincided with rapid urban growth and deepening regional disparities, particularly between the agricultural Windward Plains and the industrializing east. In 1837, the publication was renamed the Windward Plains Gazette, reflecting a shift in focus toward the specific concerns of the plains' farming communities.

    Throughout the 19th century, the Gazette maintained close ties to the landed gentry of the Windward Plains. It provided detailed coverage of crop yields, grain prices, and livestock auctions, and was widely read among estate owners and tenant farmers alike. The failed republican coup of 1856 and the subsequent Second Compact did little to alter the newspaper's editorial direction, as it continued to advocate for the interests of traditional agricultural elites within the constitutional monarchy.

    Editorial stance

    The Windward Plains Gazette is consistently described as conservative and pro-agriculture. Its editorial pages have historically supported the interests of the landed gentry, including protections for grain tariffs, opposition to guild reforms that threatened rural labor practices, and skepticism toward centralized industrial policies promoted from Havenport and Silvershield City.

    Accounts of the newspaper's political leanings note a general alignment with the Hereditary Council and the conservative wing of Asterian politics, particularly during debates over land taxation and rural infrastructure. The Gazette has been critical of universal suffrage expansions, viewing them as a threat to the traditional balance between landowners and laborers, though it has moderated some positions in the post-war period.

    Circulation and reach

    With a reported daily circulation of 12,000 copies, the Windward Plains Gazette is a medium-sized regional newspaper by Asterian standards. Its readership is concentrated in the Windward Plains, particularly around Windfield and the surrounding farming communities. The newspaper is distributed through subscription, newsagents, and direct delivery to rural estates.

    Although it is a daily, the Gazette's coverage is heavily weighted toward weekly market reports and seasonal agricultural cycles, which limits its appeal in more urbanized areas. The newspaper has maintained a stable circulation over the decades, with modest growth during periods of high commodity prices, but has faced competition from larger dailies such as the Havenport Herald in the broader regional market.

    Notable coverage

    The Windward Plains Gazette has broken several stories related to local political scandals and agricultural policy debates, though its reputation is more as a steadfast recorder of market data and regional events. Its archives are often consulted by historians studying 19th-century Astrian agrarian economics. The newspaper's annual survey of wheat and barley yields is considered a reliable benchmark for local harvest assessments.

    During the Great Border War (1912–1916), the Gazette provided extensive reporting on food supply logistics, troop movements through the Windward Plains, and the impact of conscription on rural households. Its editorial stance during the conflict was staunchly patriotic, supporting the monarchy and the war effort while criticizing the central government's handling of agricultural requisition orders.
