    The Progressive Technological Alliance (PTA) is a centrist political party in the constitutional monarchy of Asteria, founded in 2072. The party advocates for managed technological growth, digital rights, and a stronger regulatory framework for the Digital Guild. In the Election of 2075, the PTA won 30 seats in the Elected Chamber, becoming the third-largest party, and subsequently supported the People's Labor Party minority government on technology-related issues.

    History

    The Progressive Technological Alliance was founded in 2072, emerging from a coalition of academics, technology entrepreneurs, and civil libertarians concerned with the rapid growth of guild-dominated industries in Asteria. The party's formation was spurred by a series of scandals involving the Digital Guild, including allegations of data misuse and anti-competitive practices, which inflamed public debate about the unchecked power of hereditary guilds.

    The PTA contested its first general election in 2075, capitalizing on urban and younger voter dissatisfaction with the established parties. The election saw the PTA secure 30 seats in the Elected Chamber, making it the third-largest party behind the Guild Unity Party (which lost 12 seats) and the People's Labor Party. The People's Labor Party, lacking a majority, formed a minority government and entered into a confidence-and-supply agreement with the PTA on technology regulation and digital infrastructure policies.

    Ideology and platform

    The PTA positions itself as a centrist party, advocating for managed technological growth that balances innovation with social welfare and individual rights. Its platform emphasizes digital rights, including privacy protections, net neutrality, and open access to digital services, as well as stronger oversight of the Digital Guild to prevent monopolistic abuses.

    Economically, the party supports investment in digital infrastructure, education in science and technology, and meritocratic governance that reduces the influence of hereditary guild privileges. In contrast with the Guild Unity Party, which favors guild autonomy, the PTA calls for a regulatory framework that ensures transparency and competition in the tech sector. The party is generally associated with environmental reform, linking technology to sustainable development, though its specific proposals remain broadly defined.

The party's funding sources are not well disclosed, though it is believed to draw support from technology professionals in cities such as Havenport and Silvershield City.

    Electoral history

    The Progressive Technological Alliance has contested only one general election to date. In the 2075 election for the Elected Chamber, the PTA won 30 seats, representing a significant breakthrough for a new party. The election also saw the Guild Unity Party lose 12 seats, while the People's Labor Party emerged as the largest party but short of a majority.

    The PTA's success was concentrated in urban constituencies and among younger demographics, particularly in the southern coastal region and the Silvershield Basin. Regionalist parties lost ground in the Eastern Reaches amid the fallout of a failed secession, but the PTA did not directly contest those districts. The party's electoral performance positioned it as a potential kingmaker on technology-related legislation.
