    The Reform Guild Party (RGP) is a political party in Asteria formed in 2014 as a splinter from the Guild Unity Party following the Thornwood dumping scandal. The party advocates for limited environmental regulation, modernization of guild charters, and a cooperative stance with the Silvershield Environmental Commission. It holds a small but growing bloc in the Elected Chamber of the Asterian Parliament.

    History

    The Reform Guild Party emerged in 2014 as a breakaway faction from the Guild Unity Party (GUP), triggered by the Thornwood dumping scandal. That scandal involved the illegal disposal of mining waste in the Thornwood region, which caused significant environmental damage and public outcry. Within the GUP, a faction argued that the party's refusal to accept any form of environmental oversight was politically unsustainable, but leadership resisted reform. When internal efforts failed, the dissidents left to form the RGP, positioning themselves as a moderate alternative that accepted limited regulation while still defending guild interests.

    The party contested its first elections shortly after formation. Over the following years, it slowly built a presence in the Elected Chamber, drawing support from guild members and constituencies that wanted to avoid both the status quo and more radical environmentalist policies. The RGP has remained distinct from both the Guild Unity Party and the environmentalist People's Ecologist Party.

    Ideology and platform

    The RGP's ideology is described as moderate guild reformism. It advocates for limited environmental regulation, arguing that some oversight is necessary to prevent future scandals like the Thornwood incident, but opposing measures that would restrict guild productivity. The party calls for modernizing guild charters, which it views as outdated frameworks that hinder economic adaptation, particularly in technology and trade. It supports a cooperative stance with the Silvershield Environmental Commission, favouring negotiation and gradual compliance over punitive enforcement.

    On broader economic issues, the RGP aligns with traditional guild interests, such as maintaining licensing systems and protecting domestic industries, but with an emphasis on transparency and accountability. The party opposes wholesale deregulation as well as heavy state intervention. It generally supports the constitutional monarchy and the existing bicameral system, though it has occasionally expressed willingness to consider reforms to the Hereditary Council if they are part of a broader modernization package.

    Organization

    The Reform Guild Party is structurally similar to other Asterian political parties, with a party council and a leader elected by members. Its membership base is smaller than that of the Guild Unity Party, but the party has made consistent efforts to expand, particularly in urban centers and regions affected by the Thornwood scandal. The party's headquarters are presumed to be in Silvershield City, the political heart of Asteria, though no specific address is recorded in available sources.

    The RGP's parliamentary bloc in the Elected Chamber is described as small but growing. The party has not participated in any governing coalition as of 2028, but it has occasionally supported legislation on a case-by-case basis. Internal decision-making is reportedly collegial, with emphasis on consensus among its parliamentary members.

    Electoral history

    The Reform Guild Party first contested national elections in 2014 or shortly after its founding, though precise vote shares for early elections are not well documented. It secured a small number of seats in the Elected Chamber and has gradually increased its representation. In the 2028 election, the RGP retained its parliamentary presence, though the election was dominated by the coalition of the People's Labor Party and People's Ecologist Party, which formed a government under Grand Chamberlain Kaelina Marinsdotter. The RGP's bloc was not part of that coalition.

    The party has not yet been a kingmaker in any government formation, but its growing support suggests it could play a more influential role in future parliaments, especially if the balance between guild-aligned and environmental parties remains close.
