    Asterhaven (formerly known as Althar City) is the capital and largest city of Asteria, situated on the country's eastern coast at a deep natural harbour. With an estimated population of 850,000, it serves as the nation's political centre and a major port for regional trade. Originally the seat of the Althar dynasty, the city was renamed Asterhaven in 1874 following the overthrow of the monarchy. It is a hub for government institutions, including the National Archives of Asteria and the Supreme Court of Administrative Appeals.

    History

    Asterhaven originated as the seat of the Althar dynasty, a ruling family that controlled the surrounding lowlands. The earliest recorded event involving the city is the 1710 Treaty of Colden, signed between representatives of the Caldon Mountain Clans and the city, which established fixed tariffs for highland goods and banned raids, though it did not address Caldon demands for autonomous governance.[1]

    In 1809, Asterhaven's growing commercial navy blockaded the neighbouring port of Port Selene, forcing its surrender after six weeks. The Port Selene governing council was dissolved and replaced with an Asterhaven-appointed administration, and the territory was absorbed as a district of Asterhaven, retaining only limited trade privileges.[2]

    During the Unification Convention of 1847, delegates from major lowland cities convened in Asterhaven to sign the Act of Unification, which proclaimed the Kingdom of Althar and centralised taxation and military command. The Caldon Mountain Clans and the Southern Marches abstained from the convention.[3] The city remained the royal seat until 1874, when the monarchy was overthrown and the settlement was renamed Asterhaven, shedding its former name of Althar City.

    In May 1901, an estimated 3,000 Caldon protestors marched from Colden to Asterhaven demanding justice for the murder of Kira Durnik. The Asterian Reform League allied with the marchers, but the government refused to meet their demands and the protest ended without concessions after a non-violent standoff.[6] Two decades later, the Revolution of 1921 saw crowds storm the royal palace in Asterhaven, forcing King Althar to flee to Velden and leading to the declaration of the First Republic by the Asterian Reform League.[7] The transition was relatively bloodless but left the new regime with a bankrupt treasury.

    A significant later legal event was the 1929 class-action suit Tenant Farmers vs. Sylene Valley Agricultural Board, heard by the Supreme Court of Administrative Appeals located in Asterhaven. The court ruled in favour of the Board, sparking small-scale protests but no policy change.[4]

    Geography and Climate

    Asterhaven lies on the eastern coast of Asteria, at the mouth of a deep natural harbour that opens onto the Vythian Sea. The city is built on gently sloping terrain rising from the waterfront, with the Caldon Mountains visible to the west. The Sylene River, which bisects the country, empties into the sea through a marshy delta well south of the city, so Asterhaven's harbour is not directly affected by river siltation.

    The climate is temperate maritime, influenced by the sea. Winters are cool and wet, with occasional frost, while summers are mild to warm. Precipitation is distributed throughout the year, with slightly drier months in late spring. The harbour rarely freezes, allowing year-round shipping operations.

    Government and Institutions

    As the national capital, Asterhaven houses the principal institutions of the Asterian federal parliamentary republic. The Chamber of Deputies and the Council of Regions (the bicameral Federal Assembly) convene in the city, along with the offices of the Chancellor and the ceremonial President. The Supreme Court of Administrative Appeals, the highest administrative court in the country, is based in Asterhaven and heard the 1929 tenant farmers case.[5]

    The National Archives of Asteria, a major repository of historical and government records, is also located in the city.[8] It holds primary sources such as the proceedings of the Unification Convention and the official proclamation of the Absorption of Port Selene.[3][2]

    The city itself is administered by a municipal government with an elected mayor and council. Asterhaven is divided into several districts, including the historic core (former Althar City), the harbour district, and the absorbed district of Port Selene.

    Economy

    Asterhaven's economy is dominated by its role as the largest port in Asteria, handling a significant share of the country's maritime trade. The harbour supports shipbuilding, cargo handling, and associated industries such as warehousing and insurance. The city is a major hub for regional trade, connecting the Sylene Valley's agricultural output and the Caldon Mountains' mineral resources to international markets.

    Manufacturing is concentrated in the harbour district and includes food processing, metalworking, and the production of maritime equipment. The state-owned Althar Railway Company maintains its eastern terminus in Asterhaven, linking the port to inland cities such as Velden and Marlow. Tourism is a growing sector, with visitors drawn to the waterfront and historic architecture.

    Fishing remains a traditional industry, though its relative importance has declined as commercial shipping expanded. The Asterian Central Bank, headquartered in Asterhaven, manages the country's currency, the Asterian Crown.

    Demographics

    With an estimated population of 850,000, Asterhaven is the largest city in Asteria. The official language is Asterian, spoken by the vast majority of residents. The lowland population historically dominated the city, but migration from the Sylene Valley, the Southern Marches, and the Caldon highlands has increased diversity. The Caldon community, though a minority, has established cultural organisations and newspapers such as The Caldon Monthly.

    The population is concentrated in the older central districts and the former Port Selene district, with newer suburbs extending north and east along the coast. Population density is highest near the harbour and in the city centre. Census data from the post-republic period shows steady growth driven by rural-to-urban migration and the expansion of government employment.

    Culture and Landmarks

    Asterhaven's cultural institutions include the National Archives of Asteria, which houses documents spanning the pre-unification period through the republic. The Supreme Court of Administrative Appeals building, dating from the early federal period, is a notable example of neoclassical architecture. The former royal palace, stormed during the 1921 revolution, now serves as a museum and event space.

    The city's historic core retains narrow streets and buildings from the Althar dynasty era, though much was rebuilt after the 1921 revolution. The harbourfront features a mix of commercial piers, fish markets, and parkland. The Port Selene district has a distinct maritime character with its own guild halls and former council chambers.

    Annual events include the Asterhaven Harbour Festival, celebrating the city's maritime heritage, and political rallies often held at Unity Square. The city is also a centre for the Asterian Reform League's activities, with its headquarters located near the Federal Assembly.

    Transport

    Asterhaven is the eastern terminus of the state-run Althar Railway Company network, which provides passenger and freight connections to Velden, Marlow, and other inland cities. The city's main railway station is located in the central district. Road links connect Asterhaven to the national highway system, with routes running west along the Sylene Valley and north to the Northern Highlands.

    The deep natural harbour is the busiest in Asteria, handling container ships, bulk carriers, and ferries. Regular maritime services link Asterhaven to ports in Velden and Marlow, as well as to international destinations across the Vythian Sea. A small regional airport, Asterhaven International Airport (formerly Althar Field), handles domestic flights and limited regional connections.

    Public transport within the city includes a network of buses and trams operated by the municipal transit authority. The harbour district is also served by water taxis. Traffic congestion is a growing concern, and the city government has promoted cycling and pedestrian zones in the historic centre.
