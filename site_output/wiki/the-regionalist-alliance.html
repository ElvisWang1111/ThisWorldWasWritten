    The Regionalist Alliance is a political party in Asteria formed in 1965 during the Modernization Era. It advocates for greater autonomy for the regions of Thornwood and the Shattered Isles, which have historically been peripheral to the industrial and political core of the country. As of the most recent elections, the party holds eight seats in the Elected Chamber, giving it a minor but persistent voice in the national parliament. The party's platform centers on devolution of powers from the central government, including control over local resources, taxation, and infrastructure investment.

    History

    The Regionalist Alliance was founded in 1965, in the latter part of the Modernization Era that followed the Great Border War (1912–1916) and the subsequent expansion of suffrage. The party emerged from a coalition of local grievance movements in the northern taiga of the Thornwood and the storm-swept archipelago of the Shattered Isles. These regions felt neglected by the central government, which had concentrated industrial development in Silvershield City and Havenport. The reinstatement of appointed regional governors in 1972, after a period of direct central rule, further inflamed demands for local control and gave the party a clearer target for its advocacy.

    The Alliance initially struggled to gain a foothold in national politics, as the limited male suffrage before 1967 restricted its potential electorate. The extension of universal male suffrage in 1967, however, broadened the voter pool in both regions, and the party has since managed to secure a small, stable presence in the Elected Chamber. Internal records from the period are scarce, but accounts suggest the party built its early organization through town-hall meetings in mining settlements of the Thornwood and fishing villages of the Shattered Isles.

    Ideology and platform

    The Regionalist Alliance is explicitly regionalist and devolutionist. Its core demand is the transfer of significant administrative and fiscal powers from the central government to the Thornwood and the Shattered Isles. The party argues that the central parliament, dominated by representatives from the wealthier Windward Plains and the Eastern Reaches, has consistently underfunded infrastructure in the periphery and ignored the specific needs of cold-climate mining communities and island fishing economies.

    In practice, the party’s platform calls for locally controlled land-use and resource extraction policies especially important for the Thornwood's iron and rare-earth mining, and for greater authority over maritime regulation and storm-relief in the Shattered Isles. The party also advocates for a reform of the system of appointed governors, preferring elected regional councils with taxing powers. Critics within the central parliamentary majority, such as the Guild Unity Party, have characterized these proposals as a step toward secession, though the Alliance has consistently denied any separatist ambitions.

    Organization

    The Regionalist Alliance is a small party with a rudimentary organizational structure. Its base lies in the Thornwood and the Shattered Isles, where it maintains local branches in major settlements such as Thornburg and Marinersgate. The party’s national leadership is chosen by a conference of delegates from these branches, typically meeting in the town of Ironhold or occasionally in Silvershield City for parliamentary sessions.

    Funding comes primarily from membership dues and small donations from local businesses and guilds in the resource sectors. The party lacks the financial resources of larger national parties and does not own a permanent headquarters in the capital. Its representation in the Elected Chamber consists of eight members, who often coordinate with other minor parties on issues of mutual interest, though the Alliance maintains a distinct identity focused on its regional agenda.

    Electoral history

    Since its founding, the Regionalist Alliance has consistently won a small number of seats in the Elected Chamber. Electoral rolls before 1967 are incomplete, but the party’s first successful campaigns came in the 1970 general election, following the suffrage expansion. The party’s seat count has fluctuated between five and ten, with the current count of eight recorded after the most recent national poll.

    Given the first-past-the-post system used in most constituencies, the Alliance has never formed a government or participated in a coalition cabinet. Its influence is felt primarily through parliamentary questions, committee work, and occasional bargaining with larger parties when a close vote requires cross-bench support. The party has been most vocal during debates over the annual budget, arguing for increased transfer payments to its regions.

    Notable figures

    No single leader of the Regionalist Alliance has achieved national prominence outside the party’s own circles. The party’s leadership has typically come from local activists with backgrounds in mining cooperatives or fishing guilds. Names that appear in the sparse parliamentary records include several individuals who have represented constituencies in the Thornwood and the Shattered Isles, but full biographies have not been compiled.
