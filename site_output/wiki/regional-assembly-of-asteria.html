    The Regional Assembly of Asteria is the upper legislative chamber of the Asterian parliament, established in 2035 under the Fifth Compact. It replaced the absolute veto previously held by the Hereditary Council and was designed to reduce regional tensions, particularly those arising from the Eastern Reaches. The Assembly comprises 90 members elected by proportional representation, with 18 representatives from each of the five regions: the Windward Plains, the Eastern Reaches, the Thornwood, the Southern Windward Plains, and the Shattered Isles. Its primary responsibilities include oversight of regional development funds, environmental regulations, and the initiation of legislation on local matters.

    History

    The Regional Assembly was created as part of the Fifth Compact, a major constitutional reform enacted in 2035. The reform aimed to address long-standing regional grievances, especially in the Eastern Reaches, where economic disparities and limited political representation had fueled calls for greater autonomy. Under the earlier Second Compact (1856), power had been centralized in the capital, and the Hereditary Council—composed of noble families and guild heads—retained an absolute veto over legislation. This arrangement had increasingly been criticized as unresponsive to regional needs.

    The Fifth Compact dissolved the Hereditary Council's absolute veto and replaced it with the Regional Assembly, a chamber designed to give each region a direct voice in national governance. The creation of the Assembly was preceded by several years of negotiation among the Grand Chamberlain, regional governors, and representatives from the Elected Chamber. While the reform was broadly supported in the regions, it encountered resistance from conservative factions within the Hereditary Council and some guild interests, who viewed it as an erosion of traditional authority. The Assembly first convened in late 2035, holding its inaugural session at the newly renovated legislative complex in Silvershield City.

    Organization and structure

    The Regional Assembly consists of 90 members, with 18 elected from each of Asteria's five regions: the Windward Plains, the Eastern Reaches, the Thornwood, the Southern Windward Plains, and the Shattered Isles. Elections are conducted using proportional representation, a system intended to ensure that minority viewpoints and smaller communities within each region are represented. The exact electoral formula—such as the use of party lists or single transferable votes—is not specified in public records, but the system was explicitly chosen to prevent any single faction from dominating a region's delegation.

    Members serve terms of five years, coinciding with the electoral cycle of the lower house, the Elected Chamber. Unlike the Hereditary Council, which held seats by inheritance or guild appointment, all members of the Regional Assembly must be elected. The Assembly elects its own Speaker from among its members, and committees are formed to handle specific portfolios such as regional development, environmental oversight, and local legislation. The Assembly's powers are defined by the Fifth Compact: it has authority over the allocation of regional development funds, the creation and enforcement of environmental regulations, and the ability to initiate legislation on matters deemed local or regional in nature.

    Activities and role

    The Regional Assembly functions as a legislative chamber with a focus on regional and local affairs. It shares the general legislative power with the Elected Chamber and the Hereditary Council, but its specific purview includes the distribution of funds for infrastructure, education, and public works projects within each region. Environmental regulations—covering issues such as mining impact, forest management, and water rights—are also a major area of activity, reflecting the diverse geography of Asteria: from the fertile farmland of the Windward Plains to the mining zones of the Eastern Reaches and Thornwood.

    The Assembly's capacity to initiate legislation on local matters allows it to propose bills without needing approval from the Elected Chamber, though all laws still must pass both houses and receive royal assent. In practice, the Regional Assembly often serves as a forum for regional governors and local councils to bring concerns to the national stage. Its creation has been credited with reducing the frequency of regional petitions to the Grand Chamberlain, as the Assembly provides a direct institutional channel for addressing disputes. The Assembly also reviews the budgets of regional development agencies and can amend proposed allocations.

    Controversies

    The creation of the Regional Assembly itself was controversial, particularly among hereditary nobles and guild leaders who had previously held absolute veto power through the Hereditary Council. Some argued that the new chamber duplicated the work of the Elected Chamber and would slow decision-making. In the Eastern Reaches, where demands for autonomy had been strongest, the allocation of only 18 seats—equal to every other region—was criticized as insufficient given the region's population and economic contribution. Conversely, representatives from the Windward Plains worried that the Assembly would give disproportionate influence to sparsely populated regions like the Thornwood.

    Subsequent debates have centered on the Assembly's environmental powers, especially regarding mining regulations in the Eastern Reaches and logging in the Thornwood. Disputes between the Assembly and the Hereditary Council over jurisdictional boundaries have occasionally led to legislative gridlock, though the Fifth Compact includes a mediation mechanism involving the Grand Chamberlain. The proportional representation system has also been criticized for producing fragmented delegations that struggle to form stable majorities on key votes.
