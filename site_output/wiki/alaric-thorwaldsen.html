    Alaric Thorwaldsen (1929–1995), also known as the Lord of Thornburg, was an Asterian noble and politician from the Thornwood region. He served as a hereditary councilor in the Asteria|Asterian legislature and was a leading figure in the The Regionalist Alliance|Regionalist Alliance. Thorwaldsen is best known for his vocal opposition to corruption within the Thornwood Lumber Commission and for organizing a brief secessionist movement in the 1970s that sought greater autonomy for the Thornwood. His actions highlighted long-standing regional grievances within the constitutional monarchy of Asteria.

    Early life and family background

    Alaric Thorwaldsen was born in 1929 in the Thornwood, a cold taiga region of northern Asteria known for its mining settlements and extensive forests. He was the heir to the Thorwaldsen family, a noble house that held the title Lord of Thornburg and a hereditary seat on the Asteria|Hereditary Council of Asteria. Little is recorded about his early education, but the Thorwaldsen estate near Thornburg was a modest manorial holding, typical of the region's lesser nobility.

    The Thornwood had long been economically dependent on timber and mineral extraction, much of it controlled by powerful guilds such as the Thornwood Lumber Commission. During Thorwaldsen's youth, the region experienced growing discontent over the distribution of resource revenues and the influence of centralized authorities from the southern capital, Silvershield City. These conditions would later shape his political outlook.

    Political career

    Thorwaldsen entered politics as a hereditary councilor, taking his family's seat in the upper chamber of the Asterian parliament. He emerged as a vocal critic of the Thornwood Lumber Commission, which he accused of bribery, price-fixing, and exploiting local workers and landowners. His speeches in the council chamber often invoked the need to protect the Thornwood's natural resources for its inhabitants rather than distant guild interests.

    By the early 1960s, Thorwaldsen had aligned himself with the The Regionalist Alliance|Regionalist Alliance, a political faction that advocated for devolving more authority to Asteria's five regions. The Alliance drew support from the Thornwood and other peripheral areas, such as the Eastern Reaches, where similar grievances existed. Thorwaldsen used his position to introduce measures aimed at increasing local oversight of the Lumber Commission, though most were blocked by the Commission's allies in the Elected Chamber and the Crown.

    Secessionist movement

    In the early 1970s, following the reinstatement of regional governors in 1972—a reform that created new tensions between the central government and the regions—Thorwaldsen escalated his stance. He declared the Thornwood's intention to secede from Asteria and establish an independent commonwealth. The secessionist movement was brief, lasting only a few months, and never gained widespread military support. Thorwaldsen's call for a referendum was rejected by the Grand Chamberlain, and the movement collapsed after a series of arrests and the threat of economic sanctions from Silvershield City.

    Accounts differ on the precise demands of the secessionists, but contemporary reports in the Havenport Herald and other newspapers suggest that Thorwaldsen sought not full independence but a status akin to a self-governing protectorate, with control over local taxes and resource extraction. The failure of the movement did not lead to immediate reprisals; Thorwaldsen was allowed to retain his council seat, likely because the central government saw him as a manageable dissident rather than a serious threat.

    Later life and death

    After the secession attempt, Thorwaldsen continued to serve on the Hereditary Council but retreated from the forefront of regional activism. He focused on local affairs in Thornburg, including the management of the Thorwaldsen estate and participation in the Windward Plains Gazette|Windward Grain Consortium—a cooperative that marketed Thornwood lumber and minerals to southern buyers. He remained a member of the Regionalist Alliance but took no further part in separatist activities.

    Alaric Thorwaldsen died in 1995 at the age of 66. Obituaries in the The Eastern Reaches Chronicle|Eastern Reaches Chronicle and other regional papers noted his role in bringing attention to the Thornwood's economic marginalization, while centralist publications criticized his brief flirtation with secession as reckless.

    Legacy

    Thorwaldsen is remembered primarily as a symbol of Thornwood regionalism and a voice against guild corruption. His opposition to the Thornwood Lumber Commission inspired later reform movements that, in the 1990s and 2000s, led to the commission's restructuring and greater local representation on its board. Historians of Asterian politics often cite the 1970s secessionist episode as an example of the tensions created by the 1972 reinstatement of regional governors, a policy that inadvertently strengthened regionalist identities.

    Monuments to Thorwaldsen are scarce; a small plaque in Thornburg's town square commemorates his service, and his name appears in the records of the The Regionalist Alliance|Regionalist Alliance as one of its early champions. Academic studies of the movement, such as those published by Silvershield University, generally treat Thorwaldsen as a principled but ultimately unsuccessful figure whose actions foreshadowed later debates about devolution in Asteria.
