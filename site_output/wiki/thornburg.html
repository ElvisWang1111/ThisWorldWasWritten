    Thornburg, originally known as Thornburg Mining Camp, is a rugged mining town in the northern Thornwood region of Asteria. Founded in 1845 during the iron and copper boom of the 1840s, it grew into a center for mineral extraction and lumber processing, with a population of approximately 12,300 as of the latest records. The town is predominantly inhabited by guild miners and lumberjacks who speak the Thornic language. Thornburg is historically significant as the birthplace of the Thornwood Freehold Movement, a political organization advocating for regional autonomy founded in 1947.

    History

    Thornburg was established in 1845 as a mining camp during the iron and copper boom that swept the Thornwood region in the 1840s. Its early growth was driven by discoveries of rich ore deposits, attracting settlers from the southern lowlands and immigrant laborers. The settlement quickly became a hub for the Royal Guild of Miners, which organized extraction and trade, and for lumberjacks supplying timber for mine supports and construction.

    In the post-war period, Thornburg became a focal point for regional grievances. On June 24, 1947, activists including Eira Aldersdotter gathered in the town to form the Thornwood Freehold Movement, a political organization that called for devolution of power, recognition of Thornic language rights, and an end to timber exploitation by outsiders. The movement's founding charter was signed in Thornburg and soon became the dominant political force in the region, though its demands faced resistance from the central government in Havenport.[1][2]

    Later decades saw Thornburg's economy diversify slightly, but it remained primarily a mining and lumber town. The town's infrastructure, including a rail link to Ironhold and Copperhold, was upgraded during the 20th century, though it retained a frontier character.

    Geography and climate

    Thornburg lies in the northern Thornwood, a cold taiga region characterized by dense coniferous forests and rocky terrain. The town sits at an elevation of 420 meters on the banks of the Thornrun River, which provides water for mining operations and transportation. The surrounding area is part of the Spine of Aethon foothills, with scattered outcrops of iron and copper ore.

    The climate is subarctic, with long, harsh winters and short, cool summers. Average winter temperatures frequently drop below −15 °C, and snowfall can exceed two meters annually. The growing season is less than 100 days, making agriculture marginal; most food supplies are imported from the Windward Plains. The rugged geography and permafrost conditions pose challenges for mining and construction, but the region's mineral wealth has sustained settlement.

    Government

    Thornburg is governed under Asteria's local administration system, which has undergone several reforms. Following the Second Compact of 1856, the town was placed under the authority of an appointed regional governor, a system that lasted until the office was revoked in 1923. After a period of direct rule from Silvershield City, governors were reinstated in 1972, leading to tensions over local autonomy.

    The town itself elects a mayor and a town council, which oversee public services, zoning, and local regulations. However, many key functions—such as mining permits and timber rights—are controlled by guild bodies like the Royal Guild of Miners and the Thornwood Lumber Commission, which operate under national charters. The Thornwood Freehold Movement has repeatedly pushed for greater local control over these resources, but central authorities have resisted sweeping devolution. Voting rights in municipal elections have been universal since the 1967 reform, though turnout remains modest in off-cycle years.

    Economy

    Thornburg's economy is dominated by mining and lumber processing. Iron and copper are the primary minerals extracted from the surrounding hills, with ore shipped south to smelters in Ironhold and Copperhold. The Royal Guild of Miners holds a near-monopoly on extraction, controlling both labor contracts and trade routes. Timber from the Thornwood forests is harvested by the Thornwood Lumber Commission, which supplies lumber to construction markets across Asteria, particularly in Havenport and the Windward Plains.

    Smaller industries include blacksmithing, equipment repair, and a modest service sector catering to the mining population. The town has a branch of the Astrian National Bank and a local market for trade in goods. Agriculture is limited to small garden plots due to the climate, so most food is imported, contributing to higher living costs. The currency used is the Astrian Crown, which has fluctuated in value since the 1930s devaluation.

    The town's economy has faced periodic downturns linked to commodity prices and guild quotas. The Thornwood Freehold Movement has advocated for diversifying the economic base and reducing dependence on external guilds, with limited success as of the late 20th century.

The population is predominantly composed of guild miners and lumberjacks, with smaller numbers of merchants, artisans, and administrative staff.     Culture and landmarks

    Thornburg retains the character of a 19th-century mining camp, with wooden buildings, narrow streets, and a central square dominated by the Miners' Guild Hall. The town's most famous landmark is the Thornwood Freehold Memorial, a modest stone monument erected in 1948 to commemorate the founding of the movement. The original charter of the Thornwood Freehold Movement is preserved in the town archives.[1]

    Annual festivals include the Founders' Day celebration in June, marking the town's founding, and the Winter Solstice fair, which features ice sculpture competitions and miners' songs. The Thornic language has a strong oral tradition, with ballads recounting the hardships of early settlers and the political struggles of the Freehold Movement. The town has a public library, a small museum dedicated to mining history, and several bars and inns that serve as social hubs.

    Nearby natural landmarks include the Thornrun River gorge and the old copper workings at Mount Vardun, a peak visible from the town. The area is a popular destination for hikers and geologists studying the Spine of Aethon formations.
