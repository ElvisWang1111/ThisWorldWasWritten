    The Eastern Reaches Secession Referendum of 2060 was a legally recognized vote held in the Eastern Reaches region of Asteria, organized by the Asterian Sovereignty Party amid growing discontent over environmental degradation and economic neglect. The referendum, which asked voters whether the Eastern Reaches should secede from Asteria, failed with 58% voting against secession. Urban centers such as Copperhold strongly opposed secession, while rural mining areas supported it. In response, the central government under Grand Duchess Ariana Vex suspended the Eastern Reaches Regional Assembly and imposed direct rule for two years, further deepening regional tensions.

    Campaign

    The referendum was conducted over a single day in late 2060. The question presented to voters was whether the Eastern Reaches should become an independent republic. The Asterian Sovereignty Party, supported by the Eastern Reaches Autonomy League, led the campaign for a 'yes' vote, emphasizing self-determination, control over mineral wealth, and an end to perceived exploitation by the central government. Their campaign was strongest in rural mining areas such as the Thornwood periphery and the upper Serpent River settlements, where economic hardship and environmental damage were most acute.

    In contrast, the city of Copperhold—the region's largest urban center and a hub for trade, administration, and services—became the center of the 'remain' campaign. Local business leaders, moderate politicians, and the Eastern Reaches Chronicle newspaper argued that secession would disrupt trade, damage the economy, and isolate the region from national infrastructure. The central government, led by Grand Duchess Ariana Vex, formally opposed secession but did not actively campaign, instead stressing the legal consequences of leaving the union.

    Aftermath

    The central government's response was swift and controversial. Within weeks of the referendum's defeat, Grand Duchess Ariana Vex issued a decree suspending the Eastern Reaches Regional Assembly and imposing direct rule over the region for a period of two years. The decree cited the need to 'restore order and confidence' in the wake of the divisive campaign, but critics viewed it as a punitive measure against a region that had dared to question the union. The appointed governor was replaced by a commissioner from Havenport, and local administrative powers were transferred to the central ministry.

    This heavy-handed response deepened resentment in the Eastern Reaches. The Eastern Reaches Autonomy League condemned the suspension as an authoritarian overreach and accused the monarchy of ignoring the legitimate grievances that had driven the secession movement. Over the following two years, periodic protests and small-scale civil disobedience occurred in rural areas, though no major violence was recorded. The direct rule period ended in 2062, but the episode permanently damaged trust between the region and the central government. Later analyses have described the referendum and its aftermath as a turning point in Asterian regional politics, contributing to later autonomy movements in other parts of the country.
