    Asteria is a federal parliamentary republic located on the eastern side of the Varethian continent, formed in 1847 from a collection of fragmented princedoms. It is bounded by the Caldon Mountains to the west and the Vythian Sea to the east, with the Sylene River flowing through its fertile central valley. The country is a federal parliamentary republic with a ceremonial presidency, and its capital is Asterhaven. Asterian society is marked by regional identities, including the lowland Sylene Valley and the highland Caldon communities, with lingering tensions dating back to the nation's founding.

    History

    Asteria emerged from a collection of fragmented princedoms following the Unification Convention of 1847, which established a constitutional monarchy under King Althar. The new state faced immediate challenges from highland clans, culminating in the First Caldon Uprising from May 1850 to November 1851. Caldon clans, angered by their exclusion from the convention, launched coordinated attacks on lowland settlements. The Asterian army responded with a brutal campaign, burning several highland villages and capturing the clan stronghold at Mount Calmor; leaders were executed or exiled.[9]

    The Great Sylene Depression from 1870 to 1875 devastated the agricultural economy, particularly in the Sylene Valley. A combination of overproduction and declining export demand caused grain prices to fall by 40%, leading to widespread loan defaults, bank failures in Asterhaven, and a wave of landless farmers migrating to cities or the Southern Marches. The crisis eroded support for the monarchy and spurred calls for land reform.[3]

    Mounting economic grievances and republican agitation culminated in the Revolution of 1921. In February 1921, crowds stormed the royal palace in Asterhaven, and King Althar abdicated, fleeing to Velden. The Asterian Reform League declared the First Republic, a transition that was relatively bloodless but left the new regime with a bankrupt treasury.[7] The republic proved short-lived, lasting until 1933, after which the federal parliamentary system was stabilized with a ceremonial presidency.

    Geography

    Asteria occupies a roughly rectangular area on the eastern side of the Varethian continent. Its western border is defined by the Caldon Mountains, a rugged range reaching up to 2,800 m. The Sylene River flows eastward from the mountains, bisecting the country and emptying into the Vythian Sea along a marshy delta. To the north are rolling highlands with cool summers and heavy winter snowfall, known as the Northern Highlands. The eastern coastline is a mix of sandy beaches and steep cliffs, with deep natural harbors at Asterhaven and Marlow.[6]

    The central Sylene Valley is fertile and densely populated, forming the agricultural heartland. The south is a region of dry plains and scrublands known as the Southern Marches, with a Mediterranean climate.[11] The Caldon Autonomous District occupies much of the western highlands, a territory with special administrative status due to historical tensions.[1]

    Major rivers include the Sylene and the Tarn River, while notable peaks include Mount Calmor, Mount Durnik, and Storm Peak. The geography has profoundly shaped settlement patterns and economic activities, with the lowlands supporting intensive farming and the highlands focused on mining and pastoralism.

    Government and politics

    Asteria is a federal parliamentary republic. The head of state is a president elected by the Federal Assembly every six years, a largely ceremonial role. The head of government is the Chancellor, elected by the lower house. The bicameral Federal Assembly comprises the Chamber of Deputies (250 seats elected by proportional representation) and the Council of Regions (75 members appointed by regional legislatures). Regions have considerable autonomy over education, policing, and cultural affairs. Judicial power is vested in the Supreme Court and regional appellate courts.

    The political landscape includes parties such as the National Unity Party and the Caldon Front for Autonomy, reflecting regional and ideological divides. The Federal Bureau of Land Management oversees land use and conservation policy.[5]

    Tensions between the lowland center and the highland periphery have shaped governance, particularly in the Caldon Autonomous District, which was created after the First Caldon Uprising to provide limited self-rule. The district's legal framework was formalized in 1954.[1]

    Economy

    Asteria has a mixed economy with significant state ownership in transport and energy. Agriculture dominates the Sylene Valley, where wheat, barley, and orchard fruits are grown. The Sylene Valley Agricultural Board regulates farming practices. Mining in the Caldon Mountains produces iron, copper, and marble, with companies like the Caldon Ironworks and Bromby Mining Company active in the region. The Eastern Coast supports fishing, shipbuilding, and a growing tourism sector, with Asterhaven serving as a major port for regional trade.[2]

    The Southern Marches produce wine and wool, with the Southern Marches Winegrowers' Association representing local vintners. The industrial manufacturing sector is concentrated in the cities of the Sylene Valley, including Althar Institute of Technology and Althar Railway Company. The Asterian Central Bank manages the currency, the Asterian Crown.

    Persistent economic disparity exists between the wealthy coastal and valley regions and the poorer highlands and marches. The Great Sylene Depression of the 1870s left a legacy of rural poverty and migration, and debates over land reform have recurred throughout Asterian history.

    Demographics and culture

    The population of Asteria is predominantly Asterian-speaking, with regional dialects in the highlands and marches. The lowland Sylene Valley is the most densely populated area, while the Caldon Autonomous District has a distinct cultural identity rooted in clan traditions. Asterian society values modesty, pragmatism, and self-sufficiency, with a strong emphasis on local community.

    Education is provided by institutions such as the University of the Western Uplands[8] and the Althar Institute of Technology[4]. Media outlets include The Sylene Valley Herald, The Caldon Monthly, and The Eastern Coast Reporter.

    Cultural festivals and regional cuisines vary: the Sylene Valley is known for orchard fruits and wheat-based dishes, while the Southern Marches produce wine and wool. The highlands have a tradition of folk music and oral poetry. The National Archives of Asteria preserves historical records, including those of the Revolutionary period.

    Administrative divisions

    Asteria is divided into regions with considerable autonomy. Key divisions include the Sylene Valley, the Southern Marches, and the Caldon Autonomous District.{cite:src_000026,src_000027,src_000029} The Caldon Autonomous District was established to address the grievances of the highland clans after the First Caldon Uprising, with its own legal framework formalized in 1954.[1]

    Other notable locations include the cities of Asterhaven (capital), Marlow, and Port Selene.[10] The Federal Bureau of Land Management oversees federal lands, particularly in the western mountains and coastal areas.[5]

    Regional governments have authority over education, policing, and cultural affairs, leading to differences in policy and identity across the country. The Chamber of Deputies and Council of Regions represent these divisions at the federal level.
